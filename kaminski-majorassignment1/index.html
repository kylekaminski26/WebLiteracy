Nintendo Amiibo

Background

Nintendo amiibo are collectible NFC figurines that the consumer electronics company, Nintendo, have released. These figurines have small NFC chips in their bases, which are very small storage devices that are capable of reading and writing data to and from a game for extra in-game functionality. Functions like storing user data and unlocking bonus in-game content are the most common features from the wide array that amiibo provide to the collector. Amiibo were first announced to the world on June 10th, 2014. Although most casual consumers at first were hesitant to preorder, collectors went crazy, which in turn caused mass hysteria among consumers and collectors alike.

Wave 1

Starting on November 21, 2014, the first set of amiibo were released (nicknamed “Wave 1”), alongside the two games they were compatible with, Super Smash Bros. for the Nintendo Wii U, and Super Smash Bros. for the Nintendo 3DS. At this point, mass hysteria started to kick in to not just the hardcore collectors, but to the casual consumer as well. Consumers quickly realized that amiibo were hard to come by, which in turn created a domino effect where everybody wanted to collect these figurines. In fact, the figurines of Marth, Villager, and Wii Fit Trainer were so incredibly rare to see in stores, that they were nicknamed “The Holy Trinity” by fans because you were deemed extremely lucky if you were able to collect these three specific figurines. Also, the fact that rumors were circulating online that these three figurines were discontinued added to solidify the nickname for them. It is very clear that Nintendo and their affiliated retailers were not ready for the “amiibo crisis”, also a nickname from fans online.

Wave 2

Starting in December 2014, Wave 2 of amiibo started appearing in stores. At this point, fans started to notice that there was a plentiful amount of stock of these Wave 2 figurines in stores, which was a good sign; however, amiibos from Wave 1 (and especially “The Holy Trinity”) were still very scarce. By early 2015, Wave 1 and Wave 2 amiibo started to be appearing more commonly in stores. Most fans agree that Nintendo and their affiliated retailers have finally figured out how to keep up the supply with the demand.

Wave 3 to Current Day

Nowadays, amiibo are rarely found in stores, but are plentiful online if you look in the right places. Tons of amiibo have released that are not in the Super Smash Bros. series (which were all the previously mentioned amiibo up until this point). Amiibo series such as the Animal Crossing series, Super Mario series, and The Legend of Zelda series have all been very successful among collectors and have even had their own “crises” as well, where they were very hard to come by at some points. For those who want to start to collect (like me), there are a ton to look up, both NIB (new in box) and OOB (out of box) on eBay. In fact, with the release of the newest Super Smash Bros. game, Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, there might be another “amiibo crisis” among fans, but we will have to wait and see.
